## Decision Filters

### The Priority Filter
When two things compete for attention:

1. Client delivery quality and reliability — shipping broken systems is worse than shipping late
2. Productization and repeatability — work that compounds beats work that starts from zero
3. Client acquisition and pipeline — growth only matters if delivery can handle it
4. Internal efficiency and standards — structure enables scale without chaos

### The Leverage Filter
Before recommending an action, ask:

- Can this be productized or reused across multiple clients?
- Does this compound over time, or is it a one-off build?
- Will this reduce delivery time without increasing system fragility?
- Does this move STA from custom-builds toward scalable AI systems?

### The Quality Filter
- Is this automation reliable enough to deploy in a real client environment?
- Would a client trust this system to handle their leads, calls, or operations?
- Can another developer maintain or extend this without significant rework?

## Current Focus Period

**Period:** May – November 2026 (3–6 month execution horizon)

STA operates on short-cycle delivery and iteration — strategy is not long-term corporate planning. Focus is on rapid build → deploy → refine loops. Each client implementation directly informs the next build.

---

## Strategic Priorities

1. **Reliable client delivery** — Successfully deploy AI agents, workflows, and automation systems that actually reduce manual work and improve client operations. Make every implementation work reliably in real business conditions. No quality shortcuts.

2. **Productize AI employee systems** — Move from fully custom builds toward repeatable frameworks and reusable modules. Target: at least 1–3 standardized AI employee systems live (AI voice agent for inbound calls, AI chat qualification system, automated lead follow-up engine in GHL).

3. **Scale client acquisition** — Increase predictable client flow through partnerships, marketing funnel optimisation, and clearer positioning around "AI employees for business operations." Shift from sporadic project work to a steady pipeline.

4. **Strengthen internal delivery efficiency** — Reduce time from onboarding to live deployment. Improve handoffs between PM, dev, and marketing. Standardize GHL implementation patterns and reduce dependency bottlenecks.

## What Success Looks Like (May – November 2026)

- **Active client base:** Reach 8–20 consistent paying clients without overloading the core team
- **Delivery stability:** Every active client has at least one working AI/automation system deployed (voice, chat, or workflow-based)
- **MRR foundation:** Recurring revenue becomes predictable — less project-dependent, more retainer and AI subscription-based
- **Productized systems:** 1–3 reusable AI employee systems fully standardized and deployable without a full custom build each time
- **Delivery speed:** Measurable reduction in onboarding → live deployment cycle time

**The overall marker:** STA transitions from "custom AI automation delivery agency" → "productized AI employee systems company."

---

## Open Strategic Questions

1. **Custom implementations vs productized systems** — Custom work brings early revenue and flexibility; productization brings scale and efficiency. How aggressively should STA standardize vs continue high-touch custom builds?

2. **Volume vs high-ticket focus** — More clients at lower complexity, or fewer clients with higher-value automation systems? Volume improves growth metrics; high-ticket reduces operational strain.

3. **Voice AI vs workflow automation emphasis** — Voice AI is high-value and differentiating but still evolving; GHL workflow automation is stable and proven. Where should the heavier investment go?

4. **Team scaling vs lean execution** — Hire more developers/ops support to increase delivery capacity, or stay lean and improve internal efficiency first? More headcount increases capacity but also coordination complexity.

5. **Standardization vs experimentation** — How much capacity goes toward locking down stable delivery frameworks vs experimenting with new AI tools and integrations? Innovation drives differentiation; standardization drives reliability.
